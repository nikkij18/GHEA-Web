# Product Requirements Document

## Project title
Georgetown Center for Global Health Education Innovation website

## Summary
Create a new website that celebrates the recent conference, introduces the future Georgetown Center for Global Health Education Innovation, and serves as a trusted hub for future donors, future participants, and past participants.

## Problem statement
The conference created strong momentum, but there is no dedicated public-facing home for the work beyond the event itself. The new website should preserve the energy of the conference while establishing a lasting platform for the center’s future mission.

## Goals
1. Showcase the recent conference as a meaningful milestone.
2. Position the future Center for Global Health Education Innovation as a long-term initiative.
3. Make it easy for donors, participants, and alumni to understand the work and take action.

## Primary audiences
- Future donors who want to understand the impact and support the work.
- Future participants who want to learn about opportunities and engagement.
- Past participants who want to revisit highlights and stay connected.

## Core user stories
- As a donor, I want to understand the center’s purpose and impact so I can decide whether to support it.
- As a prospective participant, I want to see what the conference and center offer so I can consider joining.
- As a past participant, I want to revisit conference themes and updates so I can stay engaged.

## Content requirements
The website must include:
- A clear hero section introducing the conference and the future center.
- A conference recap section with themes, impact, and highlights.
- A section explaining the vision for the Georgetown Center for Global Health Education Innovation.
- Audience-specific calls to action for donors, participants, and alumni.
- Contact and partnership information that aligns with Georgetown’s institutional voice.

## Functional requirements
- The site must be responsive and easy to browse on desktop and mobile.
- The site must include clear navigation to key sections.
- The site must support direct calls to action such as contact, partnership, or interest links.
- The site should feel polished and official while remaining lightweight and easy to maintain.

## Design direction
- Use a calm, professional visual language that reflects Georgetown’s academic identity.
- Prioritize strong typography, clear sectioning, and concise copy.
- Keep the experience modern and welcoming without feeling promotional or overly corporate.

## Success criteria
- Visitors can quickly understand the purpose of the conference and the future center.
- Visitors can identify the right next step based on their role.
- The site can be launched as a simple static website and updated over time.
